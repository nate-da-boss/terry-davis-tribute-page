Terry A. Davis - TempleOS Creator

Welcome to the tribute page for Terry A. Davis, programmer and creator of TempleOS.

Biography

Terry A. Davis (1969-2018) was an American programmer who created TempleOS, a biblical-themed operating system.

He started development in 2003 after God told him to build a temple. Originally called "J Operating System", it was later renamed TempleOS.

Davis wrote everything himself - the kernel, compiler, graphics libraries, and applications.

Contributions

  • Created TempleOS - a complete operating system from scratch
  • Designed HolyC programming language (a variant of C)
  • Built a 16-color 640x480 VGA graphics system
  • Implemented a 3D graphics engine for games
  • Wrote a flight simulator and many other programs
